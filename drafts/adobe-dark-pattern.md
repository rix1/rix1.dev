Adobe makes it very easy to buy more licenses for your team.

Removing one was different.

## Timeline

08:40: Start.

Search the UI for ways to cancel. One or two minutes.

Search Google and realize the UI search was useless. There is no way of
removing licenses without contacting support. One or two minutes.

08:48: Contact support.

08:49: Chatbot questions done.

08:50: Human chat. Questions, reasons, and so on.

08:55: "I have to inform you about cancellation fee..."

08:58: "To avoid the cancellation fee, you have a cancellation window of one
month in September." The suggestion is that I should come back then.

09:03: Haggling. They offer me a switch and urge me to avoid the cancellation
fee.

09:07: Done. Please fill out this survey to help us improve.

In total I spent 30 minutes and 160 NOK to cancel one license. My time is worth
more than this, and the company is doing itself a disservice by not giving
people the freedom to manage their licenses themselves. The brand is hurt, and I
am more likely to try a competitor in the future.

This is not feedback to the person I talked to. He did a good job. But I
consider the license cancellation design extremely shady. As a software engineer
and product manager, I recognize several dark patterns in the way this is set
up. It is a textbook example of making adjustment difficult in order to keep
recurring revenue going:

- It is extremely easy to buy extra licenses, with no self-service way of
  canceling them.
- Cancellation fees are hidden until 28 minutes into a conversation.
- Cancellation windows are not clearly communicated in the UI.

Give people the tools they need for managing their licenses. There are many ways
to make money and provide a good experience at the same time.

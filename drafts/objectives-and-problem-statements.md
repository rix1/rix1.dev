## Measure what matters

I watched John Doerr's TED talk and saw a summary of his book.

Objectives should be:

- Significant
- Concrete
- Action-oriented
- Inspirational

Set an audacious goal that inspires you.

Good key results should be:

- Specific and time-bound
- Aggressive yet realistic
- Measurable and verifiable

Match the goal with three to five key results. They should be a mix of quality
and quantity results.

Then measure and track progress. Check in weekly, monthly, or quarterly, and use
a simple traffic-light model:

- Green, 70-100%: continue
- Yellow, 30-70%: make a recovery plan
- Red, 0-30%: recover or replace

The important reminder is that goals are servants to our purpose, not the other
way around.

## Defining problems

I find vague success criteria frustrating. For example:

> Goal: Make it easier to input cost models for new installers.

This lacks both input data about how the situation works today and a tangible
success criterion. Here are two ways I would formulate it differently.

## Broad problem statement

**Situation:** As of June 2019, onboarding new installers takes X days on
average. This includes creating accounts, signing documents or contracts,
creating and publishing a cost model, and being assigned their first project.

**Objective:** It should take no more than three hours to onboard an installer.

This would make it possible to analyze and identify the biggest bottlenecks in
the process, both before and after creating the cost model. In addition, when
the first installer was onboarded in less than three hours, we would know that
we had succeeded and could move on to other work.

## Narrow problem statement

**Situation:** As of June 2019, it takes installers on average five hours and
three phone calls from the moment they receive admin privileges until their first
cost model is live.

**Objective:** It should take no more than 15 minutes and no phone calls for a
new installer to publish the initial cost model.

This includes input data and a success criterion that ensures business value
without being specific about implementation details.

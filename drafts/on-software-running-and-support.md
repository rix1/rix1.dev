Writing software is not hard, but it can certainly be tough. Running a marathon
is not hard either: put in enough hours and almost anyone can do it. But for
most people it is really, really tough.

I feel bad when reviewing PRs after someone has pushed through an enduring
effort. At that point they do not need someone picking at them. I guess this is
the tradeoff for creating good and maintainable code, but it still feels wrong.

It would be better if I was not picking so much as supporting. Helping them
reach the finish line.

It is important not to pick at someone after an enduring effort. That mostly
serves to destroy morale.

I also feel somewhat disloyal when prioritizing a thorough review of side
projects like these while there are unfinished tasks on my own plate. But I will
sacrifice finishing my own tasks for showing support to teammates every time.

What can wait, and what should we do now?

I guess this is the only way to make things better, but it comes at a cost of
potentially stressing relationships. What if, instead of picking at someone
after 35 kilometers of running, you joined them? You do not need to be running.
There can be enormous help in just cycling up next to them, spending time with
them, and showing support.

I believe it would be of great benefit to be able to visualize my life.

I am not sure what the original spark was, but I was listening to the audiobook
_You're It_, which talks about a lot of different historic events. Maybe that is
what triggered me.

Life is bound by time. Life is also bound by others. Whatever I come up with,
time would have to be represented in some way. Does it make sense to do lookups
based on time? What about lookups based on places?

At the surface, time and place seem strongly connected. At a given point in
time, my body will be located at a specific place within Earth's coordinate
system. But diving into this topic, I quickly realized that my mind will always
wander independently of my physical location. Where am I emotionally?
Intellectually? What are my ambitions? What inspires me? What am I thinking
about?

All of these questions are potential locations that I discover and occasionally
revisit throughout my life. Should these locations be easy to look up? How do I
capture and structure this? Initially this seems quite daunting, so I am putting
a pin in it for now.

Having established time as an important feature, I was thinking of visualizing
this as a spiral. The spiral could start from the center and grow outwards. I
like this because it will grow in size as my age increases, which works as a
metaphor for wisdom or experience. This also works well with the idea that you
are always at the end of your life.

It fails, however, to capture a second idea: that your perception of time is
relative to your age. To satisfy that idea, it might be better to draw the
spiral the other way around, from the outside in. This way the current year
would have a smaller radius than the previous one, indicating that each year
contributes less and less to your perception of time.

This might be a distraction, but something tells me that a really good
visualization can fit multiple scenarios accurately. Maybe it can even afford
reasoning or discovery based on the viewer's existing knowledge.

Before I fall asleep, here are my ideas so far:

- Draw life as a spiral from the center and outwards.
- Each revolution is one year, starting around 1 o'clock on June 27, 1990, and
  growing counterclockwise outwards.
- It could be hard to count all the lines, which is needed to do efficient
  lookups on time. To counter this, lines could be grouped in fives, maybe with
  color, stroke weight, or something else.
- It could be beneficial to isolate periods in order to do further lookups on
  those. Spring, for example. The visualization could make it possible to
  isolate periods quite easily as long as the period is shorter than a year. I
  am not sure how well this works with multi-year periods.
